RATS FURTHER OUT, N MORE RATS

1. Dark outline on every limb — biggest bang for zero effort
  A ColorRect 4px larger on all sides, show_behind_parent = true as a child of the limb rect. It follows jiggle
  automatically. Gives every limb a crisp ink border that makes the color read 2× stronger and makes them look
  intentional rather than placeholder. This is the single change that separates "colored rectangles" from "designed game
   pieces."

  2. Chassis concentric rings
  Instead of the flat solid disc, build the chassis visual from 3 stacked rects: a dark outer ring, a mid-tone main body
   (chassis color), and a small bright inner core. Looks mechanical, communicates mass at a glance — heavy chassis =
  thick dark ring, light chassis = thin ring with big bright core.

  3. Tip strike marker
  A small bright square (8×8 or so) at the very end of each limb — white or team-colored. When the bey spins it traces a
   glowing circle showing exactly where the hitbox is. Connects the art to the physics in a way that feels designed, not
   accidental.

  4. RPM-reactive limb brightness
  At high RPM, lerp the limb rect color toward a brighter/lighter version. At low RPM, let it dull. Ties the visual
  energy directly to the mechanical state. The BeyDropShadow glow already does this for the chassis — this extends the
  language to the arms.
